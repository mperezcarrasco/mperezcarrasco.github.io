Hello Everyone! I'm

Manuel Perez-Carrasco

I am MSc. in Computer Science from University of Concepción and former research scholar at the Institute for Applied Computational Sciences (IACS), Harvard University. I am interested in different topics, mostly related with computer vision and time series analysis. Take a look at my portfolio for more information about my work.

About

I was born in Chillán, a small city in the south of Chile. I lived there until I got into the University of Concepción (UdeC), and I moved to Concepción. I did my undergrad in Industrial Engineering, but I still remember and Artificial Intelligence talk given by Professor John Atkinson when I was in my second year. Since then, I got passionate about Computer Science and its scopes. Every time I could, I took CS courses both in University and MOOCs.

In my penultimate year of Engineering, I won a fully-funded scholarship to hold my MSc. in Computer Science at UdeC. Pursuing this program I was advised by Guillermo Cabrera-Vives and co-advised by Pavlos Protopapas. With their support, I was able to do an honor research undergrad thesis (currently published at PASP) and my master thesis (winner of the best student paper award at Astroinformatics2019 conference).

I am currently working in different research projects and I am also serving as a Lecturer of two Machine Learning courses for the Industrial Engineering Department of UdeC.

What moves me

I think the work is important because is how you retribute to the world what the world does for you. Therefore, while you most love what you do, more love you give to the world and the people who live in it.
Thinking what is the best way I give back to the world, I concluded something that can be summarized in three words: apply, generate and disseminate. Applying is the best way to learn acquire knowledge. When some amount of knowledge was acquired it is possible to materialize new ideas, thereby generating new knowledge. However, none of these words have sense if you are not capable to disseminate what you learned and make it available to the world.
This is what my motto has been become.

However, as the life is made up of different things, I have different hobbies such as dancing, surfing, cycling and playing drums.
