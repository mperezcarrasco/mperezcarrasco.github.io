Hello Everyone! I'm

Manuel Pérez Carrasco

I am MSc. in Computer Science from University of Concepción and former research scholar at the Institute for Applied Computational Sciences (IACS) at Harvard University. I am interested in different topics, mostly related to deep learning applied to computer vision and time series analysis. Take a look at my portfolio for more information about my work.

One day, a person I admire a lot taught me: "Have big dreams, work very hard, and trust that destiny will take you on the best path to them". That's what I think.

About

I am a Machine Learning Engineer currently working in different collaborations and research projects related to deep generative models, domain adaptation, and novelty detection algorithms applied to astronomical images and light curves, EEG signals from biomedical devices, and cancer genomic sequences. I am also co-lecturer of two Machine Learning courses for the Industrial Engineering Department of Universtity of Concepción (UdeC).

In my penultimate year of Engineering, I won a fully-funded scholarship to hold my MSc. in Computer Science at UdeC. Pursuing this program I was advised by Guillermo Cabrera-Vives and co-advised by Pavlos Protopapas. With their support, I was able to do an honor research undergrad thesis (currently published at PASP), and my master thesis (winner of the best student paper award at Astroinformatics2019 conference).

I was born in Chillán, a small city in the south of Chile. I lived there until I got into UdeC, and I moved to Concepción. I still remember an Artificial Intelligence talk given by Professor John Atkinson when I was in my third year of Industrial Engineering. Since then, I got passionate about Computer Science and its scopes. Whenever I have can, I took CS courses both in University and MOOCs. I define myself as a lifelong learner.

What moves me

I think the work is important because is how we retribute to the world what the world does for us. Therefore, while we most love what we do, more love we give to the world and the people who live in it.
One day, thinking what is the best way I give back to the world, I concluded something that can be summarized in three words: apply, generate and disseminate. Applying is my best way to learn. When some amount of knowledge is acquired it is possible to materialize new ideas, thereby generating new knowledge. However, none of these words have sense if I am not capable to disseminate what I learned and make it available to the world.
Since then, every project I did, every class I taught, and every challenge I accepted, has been thinking in those three words as my motto.

What I love

My family are by far the most important persons in my life, but as some people say "friends are the family we choose", I always been treated my friends as a part of my family.
I am this kind of person that gets energized sorrounded by other people, either hearing and sharing problems, ideas or having just casual conversations. I think this has made me capable to work with people from different areas and different perspectives of the world.
